16 Psyche

One of the most intriguing targets in the main asteroid belt, 16 Psyche is a giant metal asteroid, about three times farther away from the Sun than is the Earth. Its average diameter is about 140 miles (226 kilometre) – about one-sixteenth the diameter of Earth’s Moon or about the distance between Los Angeles and San Diego. Unlike most other asteroids that are rocky or icy bodies, scientists think the M-type (metallic) asteroid 16 Psyche is comprised mostly of metallic iron and nickel similar to Earth’s core.

3D printing

3D printing, also called additive manufacturing, is a family of processes that produces objects by adding material in layers that correspond to successive cross-sections of a 3D model. Plastics and metal alloys are the most commonly used materials for 3D printing, but it can work on nearly anything from concrete to living tissue.

Caridina Ravisankarani

Caridina Ravisankarani, a new shrimp species, discovered in India A new species of shrimp has been discovered in the Island of Andaman and Nicobar. The species, Caridina Ravisankarani was first collected in June and November 2018 and January 2019 from a limestone cave (CN2) on Interview Island, Andaman, and the Nicobar Islands. Caridina Ravisankarani Caridina Ravisankarani is a cave-adapted species and is closely related to Caridina typus H. Milne Edwards, Caridina villadolidi Blanco, and Caridina jeani Cai. However, on analysis, it was found to possess specific characters of its own and distinctly differs from C. typus, which makes it a new species. Caridina Ravisankarani is also the first report of atyid shrimps from the cave. The species has been named after renowned ornithologist and conservation biologist Dr. Ravi Sankaran, who pioneered cave studies in the Andaman and Nicobar Islands. Features Caridina Ravisankarani can be identified through its short rostrum (a beaklike projection) with an edentulous upper margin and ventral margin. It has two-minute teeth situated at the distal part; outer antennular flagellum with 16 segments at the basal part swollen. It also has highly reduced pigmentation of the eyes. Furthermore, both the male and female species are entirely translucent, with mild yellowish spots overall, more on carapace (hard upper shell). When the species were collected during June and December -January, the deep and shallow pools in the cave, it was found that the females had as many as 1300 eggs. The shrimps were found under rocks, boulders, and crevices in the pools. The newly discovered shrimp species are also photophobic, as they quickly darted in the opposite direction when exposed to the light source. These were collected from a stream, sourced through the percolation of rainwater, which reduces during the post-monsoon months.
